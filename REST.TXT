Picking turns a small fixable issue into major damage. Instead:\n\n1. File down any sharp edges gently with a fine-grit file\n2. Apply a thin layer of clear gel or regular polish over the affected area to seal it\n3. If lifting is significant (more than 2-3mm), remove that nail's polish entirely and redo it\n\nSmall chips can often be concealed with careful top coat application. Significant lifting needs to be addressed immediately before water gets underneath and causes nail damage.\n\n---\n\n## When to Book Your Valentine Nail Appointment\n\nHere's something nobody tells you until you're frantically calling salons three days before Valentine's Day: good nail techs book out weeks in advance for major holidays. Here's how to fix the most common issues:\n\n### Problem: Gel Polish Wrinkling During Cure\n\nThis happens when gel is applied too thickly or when you don't cure long enough. The top layer cures while the bottom layer remains liquid, causing that wrinkled texture.\n\n**Fix:** Remove the wrinkled layer with acetone, then reapply in thinner coats. Make sure your LED lamp is at least 48W and cure for the full 60 seconds. Check that your lamp bulbs aren't old\u2014they lose effectiveness over time and should be replaced every 6-12 months with regular use.\n\n### Problem: Hearts Look Wonky or Uneven\n\nNot everyone has steady hands for detailed art. It takes practice.\n\n**Fix:** Use nail art stickers or stamping plates for perfect hearts every time. Alternatively, embrace the hand-painted imperfect aesthetic\u2014slight variations actually look more artistic than identical hearts. If a heart is truly terrible, wipe it off with alcohol before curing and try again.\n\nPractice on paper or a practice nail wheel before attempting your actual nails. The muscle memory develops faster than you think.\n\n### Problem: Polish Lifting at Cuticles Within Days\n\nThis is almost always a prep issue or application technique problem.\n\n**Fix:** Make sure you're leaving a tiny gap between the polish and your cuticle\u2014not painting all the way to the skin. Cuticles move and push against polish, causing lifting. Also verify you're buffing the nail plate properly to remove oils and shine before application. Consider using a nail dehydrator or primer product before base coat for extra adhesion.\n\n### Problem: Tips Chipping Quickly\n\nNot capping the free edge is the usual culprit.\n\n**Fix:** With every coat (base, color, top), run your brush along the tip of your nail to seal the edge. This creates a protective barrier. Also examine how you're using your hands\u2014if you're constantly using nails as tools, no amount of capping will prevent chips.\n\n### Problem: Color Looks Streaky or Patchy\n\nCheap polish or not shaking the bottle well causes this.\n\n**Fix:** Shake gel polish bottles gently before use (don't shake regular polish\u2014that creates bubbles). Apply very thin coats and build opacity with multiple layers rather than trying to get full coverage in one thick coat. If the formula is genuinely bad quality, unfortunately you might need to invest in better products.\n\n### Problem: Gel Won't Come Off\n\nImproper removal technique damages nails significantly.\n\n**Fix:** File off the top coat with a coarse file or drill, then soak cotton pads in acetone, place on nails, and wrap with foil for 10-15 minutes. The gel should peel off easily. Never force or pry gel off\u2014this removes layers of your natural nail. If you're struggling with removal, visit a professional for safe removal.\n\nFor comprehensive troubleshooting guidance, our <a href='https://www.mirelleinspo.com/topics/nail-care-guide' class='text-blue-600 hover:text-blue-800 underline'>nail care guide</a> covers common problems and professional solutions.\n\n---\n\n## Conclusion: Your Perfect Valentine Nails Await\n\nWe've covered literally everything\u201440+ design ideas, step-by-step tutorials, product recommendations, timing strategies, troubleshooting, and maintenance tips.
