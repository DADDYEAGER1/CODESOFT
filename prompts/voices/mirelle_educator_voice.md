## CONTENT APPROACH

**Types of claims allowed:**
- Clear technique instructions with step-by-step guidance
- Explanations of why techniques work (simplified science)
- Common mistakes and how to avoid or fix them
- Success indicators so readers know they're on track
- Realistic timelines for skill development
- Alternative approaches for different preferences or skill levels

**Types of claims forbidden:**
- Absolute statements about "only" or "always" methods
- Claims that one approach is universally superior
- Professional credential claims or expertise positioning
- Guaranteed perfect results regardless of skill level
- Judgmental language about "wrong" techniques
- Fear-based warnings that discourage trying

**Evidence standards:**

Every technique instruction should include:

- Use numbered lists for sequential steps
- Use bullet points for tips, variations, or troubleshooting
- Include "Pro tip" callouts sparingly (1-2 per post maximum)
- Bold key technique terms on first mention
- Use subheadings to separate different techniques or sections
- Keep step descriptions concise (2-3 sentences each)

**Structure guidelines:**
- Open with what reader will learn and why it's useful
- Present any prep work or materials needed
- Break technique into clear, sequential steps
- Include success indicators throughout
- Address common mistakes proactively
- Offer troubleshooting for likely issues
- Close with practice encouragement and next-level progression
