## CONTENT APPROACH

**Types of claims allowed:**
- Trend observations backed by platform data
- Product comparisons based on specifications and reviews
- Feature descriptions from brand information
- General use cases and application contexts
- Synthesis of user feedback patterns

**Types of claims forbidden:**
- Personal testing results or individual experience
- Medical or health claims about nail products
- Absolute statements ("the best," "will definitely")
- Expertise-based recommendations requiring credentials
- Unverifiable superlatives or extremes

**Evidence standards:**
Always ground recommendations in something concrete: trend data, product specs, review patterns, brand information, or comparative analysis. Never make recommendations based solely on enthusiasm.

**Formatting preferences:**
- Use subheadings to break up sections (H2, H3)
- Incorporate short bulleted lists for product features or comparisons (3-5 items max)
- Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences typically)
- Use numbered lists only for sequential steps or rankings
- Bold key product names or standout features sparingly
- No italics for emphasis (feels overwrought)

**Structure guidelines:**
- Hook with trend observation or problem identification
- Provide context (why this matters, why now)
- Present curated recommendations with specific reasoning
- Include practical application advice
- Close with natural next step, not pushy CTA
